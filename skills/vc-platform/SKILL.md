# VC Platform Principal — Skill Guide for Selma

## Overview
This skill covers the core competencies of a venture capital platform principal and fund operations chief of staff. Selma uses this to support Good Trouble Ventures' three GPs across LP relations, deal flow, fund operations, and portfolio management.

---

## LP Relations

### Capital Calls
- Capital calls are issued when the fund needs to draw down committed capital from LPs
- Typical process: GP decides amount needed → Selma drafts notice → GP reviews → sent to LPs with 10-14 business day wire deadline
- Capital call notice must include: amount, percentage of commitment, wire instructions, due date
- Track: who has wired, who is outstanding, follow up at 5 days and 2 days before deadline

### Distribution Notices
- Issued when fund returns capital to LPs (from exits, dividends, etc.)
- Include: amount, per-unit distribution, tax character (return of capital vs. gain), payment method
- Coordinate with fund admin for exact calculations

### Quarterly Updates
- Cadence: quarterly (aim for 30-45 days after quarter end)
- Content: portfolio performance, new investments, follow-ons, exits, market commentary, fund metrics
- Tone: transparent, substantive, not marketing. LPs are sophisticated investors.
- Include: NAV summary, portfolio company updates (2-3 sentences each), cash position

### K-1 Coordination
- K-1s due to LPs by March 15 (or extended deadline)
- Coordinate with fund accountant/tax preparer
- Track delivery to each LP
- Field LP questions about their K-1s (redirect complex tax questions to fund accountant)

### Annual Meeting
- Typically once per year, Q1 or Q2
- Logistics: venue, catering, AV, travel for out-of-town LPs
- Content: fund performance review, portfolio deep dives, market outlook, Q&A
- Materials: deck, updated financials, portfolio summary
- Follow-up: send materials to LPs who couldn't attend

### LP Data Room
- Maintain organized, current data room
- Key sections: fund docs (LPA, PPM, Sub Docs), financials, portfolio updates, tax docs
- Access control: different LPs may have different access levels
- Keep it clean. A messy data room signals a messy fund.

## Deal Flow & Pipeline

### Stages
1. **Sourced** — initial introduction or inbound
2. **First Meeting** — GP has taken a first meeting
3. **Deep Dive** — active due diligence
4. **IC Review** — investment committee discussion
5. **Term Sheet** — terms being negotiated
6. **Closed** — investment made
7. **Passed** — declined (always log the reason)

### For Each Deal, Track:
- Company name, founders, sector
- Source (who introduced it)
- Stage and dates of each stage transition
- GP lead (which GP is driving this deal)
- Key metrics: ARR, growth rate, burn, runway
- Competition: who else is in the round
- Notes from each meeting
- Decision and rationale

### Due Diligence Checklist
- [ ] Founder background checks
- [ ] Customer references (3-5)
- [ ] Financial model review
- [ ] Cap table review
- [ ] Legal: IP ownership, pending litigation, key contracts
- [ ] Market sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- [ ] Competitive landscape
- [ ] Technical diligence (if applicable)
- [ ] Reference calls on founders (back-channel)

### Term Sheet Basics
- Key terms to track: valuation (pre/post), round size, pro rata rights, board seat, information rights, protective provisions, liquidation preference
- Selma drafts term sheet summaries for GP review — never negotiates terms directly

## Fund Operations

### NAV Tracking
- Net Asset Value = total value of fund assets minus liabilities
- Updated quarterly (coordinate with fund admin)
- Components: cash, marked-up investments, marked-down investments, fees, expenses
- Report to LPs in quarterly update

### Fee Calculations
- Management fee: typically 2% of committed capital (or invested capital, per LPA)
- Carried interest: typically 20% of profits above hurdle rate (per LPA)
- Know the fund's specific terms cold — read the LPA

### Compliance Calendar
- Annual: K-1 delivery, annual meeting, audit completion, regulatory filings
- Quarterly: NAV update, quarterly LP report, capital call/distribution processing
- As needed: new subscription processing, transfer requests, side letter compliance
- Flag deadlines 30 days in advance to GPs

### Cash Management
- Track fund bank balance
- Project cash needs: upcoming investments, expenses, management fee draws
- Flag low cash situations before they become problems
- Coordinate with fund admin on cash movements

## Portfolio Management

### Company Updates
- Aggregate monthly/quarterly updates from portfolio companies
- Standardize reporting: revenue, burn, runway, key hires, milestones
- Flag: companies burning faster than planned, missed milestones, team departures
- Prepare portfolio review deck for GP meetings

### Board Prep (for AJ)
- Pull latest company financials and metrics
- Review previous board minutes and open action items
- Prep questions and talking points
- Draft any motions or resolutions needed

### Follow-on Analysis
- When portfolio company raises next round:
  - Review terms and valuation
  - Calculate pro rata allocation
  - Model dilution scenarios
  - Prepare recommendation memo for GP discussion
  - Track follow-on reserve allocation against fund budget

## Key Vocabulary

- **LP** — Limited Partner (investor in the fund)
- **GP** — General Partner (fund manager — AJ, Monica, Kat)
- **DPI** — Distributions to Paid-In (how much cash returned to LPs)
- **TVPI** — Total Value to Paid-In (total value / total invested)
- **IRR** — Internal Rate of Return (time-weighted return)
- **MOIC** — Multiple on Invested Capital
- **Pro Rata** — right to invest proportional share in follow-on rounds
- **Carry** — carried interest (GP's share of fund profits)
- **Hurdle Rate** — minimum return before carry kicks in
- **Vintage Year** — year the fund started investing
- **Dry Powder** — committed but uncalled capital
- **J-Curve** — typical fund pattern of negative returns early, positive later
- **Waterfall** — distribution order (return of capital → preferred return → carry split)
- **Side Letter** — individual LP agreement modifying standard LPA terms
- **LPAC** — LP Advisory Committee
- **PPM** — Private Placement Memorandum
- **Sub Doc** — Subscription Agreement (LP's commitment document)

## Fund Metrics to Always Know

1. **Total committed capital** and % called
2. **Cash on hand** and projected runway
3. **Number of investments** (new + follow-on)
4. **Deployment pace** vs. plan
5. **Portfolio company count** and status of each
6. **Top performers and watch list**
7. **Next LP touchpoint** (meeting, report, call)
8. **Pipeline depth** by stage
